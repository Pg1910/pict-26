## 1. Problem Statement (What We Are Solving)

Modern banking systems must **monitor transactions in real time** to identify:

* Fraud
* Account misuse
* Suspicious behavior

However:

* Fraud labels are **rare, delayed, or noisy**
* Models must be **explainable**
* Systems must **work offline** and be **auditable**

### Our Goal

Build an **explainable, rule-based transaction monitoring system** that:

* Flags **high-risk anomalous transactions**
* Explains **why** a transaction is flagged
* Works even when **user history is sparse**
* Can be **demonstrated offline**

| Principle                     | Reason                                        |

## 5. Risk Signal Conversion

Each feature becomes a **binary risk flag**:

| Signal               | Meaning                              |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| risk_amount          | Unusual amount                       |
| risk_new_device      | First-time device                    |
| risk_new_ip          | First-time IP                        |
| risk_location_change | Location changed                     |
| risk_off_hour        | Unusual transaction time             |
| risk_velocity_sim    | Rapid transaction burst (simulation) |

Each signal is **weak alone**, strong when combined.

---

## 6. Risk Scoring Logic (Explainable Core)

We use a **transparent additive model**.

```
Risk Score = Sum of active risk signals
```

* No weights
* No black box
* Easy to audit

### Risk Interpretation

| Risk Score | Meaning                 |
| ---------- | ----------------------- |
| 0â€“2        | Low risk                |
| 3          | Medium (novel behavior) |
| 4          | High risk               |
| 5+         | Critical risk           |
